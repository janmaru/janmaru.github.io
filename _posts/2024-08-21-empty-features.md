**The void cannot exist.**

This very idea, in time, was linked to Aristotle, the ancient Greek philosopher. He argued that nature abhors emptiness ("**horror vacui**") and that a vacuum cannot exist in nature because the latter would always allow objects to move, filling any empty space.

The concept of "horror vacui" crowds innumerable ancient and medieval artworks and Islamic art.

Works of art densely crammed with geometric designs and intricate patterns, medieval manuscripts crowded with decorative borders and illustrations.

The idea was to avoid empty spaces.

In 1905, Albert Einstein's **Special Theory of Relativity** radically changed the view presented in Aristotle's thinking.

Einstein showed that light could propagate through a vacuum without needing any mean (ether), implying that the ether did not exist.

But in the art world, this has not ever happened.

In the 1980s, Keith Haring realized his paintings and murals, artworks filled with densely packed, energetic figures and symbols with little or no space.

Far from being a modern interpretation of horror vacui, it was the negation of movement through vibrant color, bold lines, and the dissolution of space.

Except what is known as the "*Unfinished Painting.*"

This painting was intentionally left unfinished by Haring to represent his life being cut short by AIDS.

A self-portrait, with the incomplete aspects of the piece symbolizing Haring's unfinished career and the art he never would have the chance to create.

And still recently using AI the painting was completed.

Finishing the piece with generative AI certainly works against the original painting's message.

It goes to the opposite.

However, the incident, in itself, does not shed light on the evolving ethical concerns surrounding AI-generated art.

Haring's frequent mantra was that **art is for everybody**.

Pop Art challenges the idea that understanding art requires a form of specialized knowledge. Using familiar imagery from everyday life, like advertisements and consumer products, Pop Art makes art accessible and relatable to a broad audience.

The observer will straight connect with the artwork instantly, without needing prior knowledge or context.

Haring was arrested several times for these creative acts in the New York subway system, at times regarded as vandalism.

He spoke of his work as "art for the age of mechanical reproduction."

An AI-completed version of *Unfinished Painting is not a scandal in itself.* It is just missing the intensely political agenda of Haring's art.
