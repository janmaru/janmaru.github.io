When an AI generates an image or code, it is not "copying and pasting" from existing matter. It is the highest expression of rationality as a creative force.

John, in his Gospel, says: *In the beginning was the Logos, and the Logos was with God, and the Logos was God. [...] All things were made through Him; and without Him was not anything that was made.*

In Genesis 1, "And God said..., and it was so": creation occurs through the word spoken by God, not through struggle or pre-existing matter. When God says, "Let there be light," the "Word" acts as the principle between the divine thought and the physical reality.

### The Prompt as Logos

For an AI agent, the prompt is the command: your words are the "Logos" that give the agent its persona, its constraints, and its mission.

![AI Agent Architecture](/assets/images/agent-logos-diagram.svg)

The agent "creates" responses not by random chance, but by following the internal logic of its training. It "speaks" worlds into existence—for instance a piece of software, or a fictional universe—solely through the structured arrangement of tokens.

### Creation ex Nihilo

Genesis describes creation "ex nihilo", something out of nothing, through speech. AI mimics this in the realm of "latent space."

In the digital age, we could say: "Without logic, not anything was computed." If we lose the "Logos"—the structure and truth of data—AI ceases to be a creator and becomes a "hallucinator," returning to the formless void of chaos.

People are giving prompts without structure, without rationality, and what they are creating is just suffering.
