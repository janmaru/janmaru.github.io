You are rich when you are **alone**, you are poor when you feel **lonely**.

> *the city dumps fill*
> *the junkyards fill*
> *the madhouses fill*
> *the hospitals fill*
> *the graveyards fill*
> *nothing else*
> *fills.*

Charles Bukowski in "Love Is a Dog from Hell" exposes human failure and suffering with common words—dumps, junkyards, madhouses, hospitals, graveyards—those things that keep filling up but do not fill our inner void. If you enter a relationship to fill a void, you aren't bringing love; you're bringing **hunger**. Just a brutal reminder of the "mass production" of misery.

The poem is called: *Alone with everybody*.

### The Capacity to Love

But the ability to be alone is the capacity to love. If your happiness is a gift you give yourself, it cannot be stolen when someone walks out the door. Only those people who are capable of being alone are capable of love, of sharing, of going into the deepest core of another person—without possessing the other, without becoming dependent on the other, without reducing the other to a thing, and without becoming addicted to an **idea**.

Only those who don't need someone can truly see someone.

### Absolute Freedom

That is the absolute **freedom**, because if the other leaves, their happiness cannot be taken by the other, because it is not given in the first place. Loneliness is a negative state: the other is missed and you start searching and seeking for the other. Aloneness is immensely beautiful.

Aloneness is our human nature. You were born alone, you will die alone. Anything else is simply a **misunderstanding**.
