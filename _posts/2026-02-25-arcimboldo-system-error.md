Giuseppe Arcimboldo was the original "system error" of the Italian Renaissance. During the Renaissance, Arcimboldo's work was often referred to as "capriccio," a kind of whim or fantasy. While everyone else was painting perfect human anatomy, he was "glitching" the human form by assembling it out of turnips, fish, and books.

### The Poetic Blue Screen

There is a poetic link between a mosaic and a blue screen of death. A BSOD happens when the computer can no longer translate its "data" into a functional "output." It's the moment our mosaic falls apart.

### When Recursion Meets Art

If the `arcimboldi_mosaic.py` script were to enter a recursive loop—for example, trying to use a mosaic image as a tile for that mosaic—it would eventually run out of memory.

But it wants not. Art, just a single time, overwhelms determinism.
